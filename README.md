# Exploratory-Data-Analysis-EDA-
# The EDA revealed critical behavioral patterns driving customer churn.

# Overall Churn
26.5% churn rate indicates moderate customer loss
This is significant enough to affect long-term profitability. Businesses must actively monitor and reduce churn.

# Demographic Insights
- Gender showed no strong influence on churn
- Customers without partners or dependents churn more

# Interpretation:
- Customers with family ties may have more stable usage patterns.

# Tenure (Most Critical Factor)
- Highest churn occurs within first 6–12 months
- Long-term customers rarely churn

# Key Insight:
- The early customer lifecycle is the most vulnerable stage

# Contract Type
- Month-to-month → 42.7% churn (highest)
- Two-year → 2.8% churn (lowest)

# Interpretation:
- Long-term commitment strongly improves retention.

# Payment Method
- Electronic check → highest churn (~45%)
- Automatic payments → lowest churn

# key Insight:
- Convenience plays a major role in retention.

# Overall Conclusion
- Churn is primarily driven by:
- Poor early experience
- Lack of commitment (short contracts)
- Payment friction
- NOT by demographics.
